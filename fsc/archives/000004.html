April 14, 2003

Free Software Club is starting from now!

We are going to start a Free Software Club in YMCA. To be a formal club of YMCA, we need at least five member to register it. For now, we have two members already. One of the members is an active developer of the Taiwan Free Software Community and he used to be an instructor of Red Hat Certified Engineer Courses. Another one is Rick, he is the well known person of computers area in YMCA. We are disappointed there is not any quality computer course in YMCA, so would like to start by ourselves. Basically, we aim to advocate Free Software in the campus, study and share our knowledges and have fun as well.

If you interested, please read the club's charter below:


The YMCA International College Free Software Club's Charter

The Free Software Club is the premiere intellectual forum on campus. We strive to provide stimulating lectures as well as open discussions of free software ranging from esoteric to pragmatic topics, and all points in between.

The Free Software Club's signature event is it's Club "Happy Hours", held at least once a month and open to any member of the Free Software community.

During these meetings the club discusses a variety of topics, mostly based on free software advocacy or research and topics established the week before. At other times, the club will discuss current events and policy concerns, all the while maintaining a strong foothold in Free Software.

The club also tries to provide interesting and topical presentations by YMCA faculty or visiting lecturers, which address current concerns either in the YMCA or the world around us.

We also sponsor events such as barbecues and other get togethers.


We welcome you guys joining us, if you basically agree with the draft of our charter, please leave a message with you email address or phone number or contact to Andrew Lee by his email:

If you have any other questions and suggestion are welcome.

Hope to to have fun with you.

Cheers

Andrew Lee
