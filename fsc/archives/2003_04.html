Draft Charter

Our goals

"Free software'' is a matter of liberty, not price. To understand the concept, you should think of "free'' as in "free speech,'' not as in "free beer.''
Free software is a matter of the users' freedom to run, copy, distribute, study, change and improve the software. More precisely, it refers to four kinds of freedom, for the users of the software:


  • The freedom to run the program, for any purpose (freedom 0).

  • The freedom to study how the program works, and adapt it to your needs (freedom 1). Access to the source code is a precondition for this.

  • The freedom to redistribute copies so you can help your neighbor (freedom 2).

  • The freedom to improve the program, and release your improvements to the public, so that the whole community benefits (freedom 3). Access to the source code is a precondition for this.


Through the joint effort of this non-profit club, we hope the notion of free software will become widespread in the near future. As such, creative work by engineers, scientists, and educators would be distributed in the form of free software. As a result, their work would become a growing organism, which would encourage the exchange of knowledge and the removal of barriers.

妨害電腦使用 將處徒刑

製作病毒程式或以電子郵件灌爆別人信箱 受刑法規範

記者宮能慧／台北報導

立法院司法委員會昨 (24)日初審通過刑法部分條文修正草案，增訂妨害電腦使用罪專章，未來製作電腦病毒程式導致他人損害，可處五年以下有期徒刑，或科或併科新台幣20萬元以下罰金，無故以電子郵件灌爆別人信箱，可處三年以下有期徒刑，或科或併科10萬元以下罰金。

為因應電腦及網路世界來臨，行政院與司法院因而提案修正刑法部分條文，將電腦與高科技犯罪行為納入規範，初審通過者包括新增妨害電腦使用罪專章，專門規範攻擊電腦或網路的犯罪，例如無故輸入他人帳號密碼、破解使用電腦的保護措施、或利用電腦系統的漏洞入侵他人電腦或相關設備，處三年以下有期徒刑。

草案規定，無故取得、刪除或變更他人電腦或相關設備的電磁紀錄，也就是電子檔案等重要資訊，導致公眾或他人損害者，可處五年以下有期徒刑；無故以電腦程式或其他電磁方式干擾他人電腦或其相關設備，導致公眾或他人損害，可處三年以下有期徒刑。電腦駭客癱瘓網路或故意用電子郵件灌爆別人信箱所造成的損害也都適用本項條文。

若對公務機關電腦或相關設備犯上述行為者，還將加重其刑至二分之一。但此類行為都是告訴乃論，也就是需要受害人提出告訴才受理偵辦；製作專供妨害電腦使用罪的電腦程式，也就是電腦病毒程式，供自己或他人犯罪，導致公眾或他人損害，可處五年以下有期徒刑、拘役或科或併科20萬元以下罰金。

April 23, 2003

Taiwan copyright laws seen as unreasonable

Taiwan News, Staff Reporter / By Lisa Wang

Student groups yesterday voiced strong opposition to the ongoing revision of the country's copyright laws, saying that some of the major amendments were unreasonable and that the government was simply acting under pressure from the United States.

Legislators are scheduled to review amendments to the copyright law today, ahead of the release by a local U.S. office of an annual special 301 watch list later this month.

"The government is under pressure from the U.S. to improve the nation's IPR environment," said Hsu Pei-ran (許沛然), spokesman for a student alliance against the revision. "Taiwan is making too many concessions on the issue."

"The revision comes at a sensitive time when the Office of the U.S. Trade Representative is set to release the annual 301 watch list," he added.

An official from the Intellectual Property Office (智財局) admitted that the IPR issue had been used as a bargaining chip by U.S. representatives in trade negotiations between the two nations.

The student alliance is comprised of dozens of cultural and environmental groups from Taiwan's colleges, including College Forum of National Taiwan University and Focus on Globalization.

"The proposed revisions, which include conditions for wider public prosecution, are too strict and are unacceptable to Taiwanese people," Hsu said.

Some powerful U.S. interest groups, such as International Intellectual Property Alliance, have long urged that as part of efforts to curb rampant IPR violations, persons who breach the copyright law should be subject to public prosecution, Hsu said.

But he further explained that World Trade Organization member countries are not required to bring copyright law violations under the umbrella of public prosecution.

"The law in Taiwan is not that strict as it was modeled on similar legislation in most European countries. Once students do not distribute or sell pirated CDs, they have no need to worry about the law," said Robin Lee (李瑞斌), CEO of Taiwan's International Federation of the Phonographic Industry unit, yesterday.

"The revision does not purport to service the U.S. Most members of the IFPI are local record companies," said Lee.

Taiwan's record industry has sustained about 20 percent loss in sales every year since 1997, according to the IFPI official. Local music companies recorded less than NT$5 billion in sales in 2002, compared to NT$12 billion in 1997.

"The rule will hinder the circulation of information and knowledge as most students cannot afford to buy original material. It is quite common on campuses for students to use photocopied text books and CDs burned by classmates," said Hsu, who is currently a journalism major at National Chengchi University.

Multinational record companies and large mainstream music studios, rather than music producers or underground musicians, will be the biggest beneficiaries of the revised law, he told the Taiwan News.

The alliance is set to stage a protest in front of Ministry of Justice today.

為什麼要自由軟體?

Why Free Software?

這個世界上多少人為了自由而戰, 為了自由而死; 中國有句話說: 不自由, 毋寧死
這個問題簡單的想就是你(妳)想不想要"自由"? 我想你(妳)該不會給回答說不想要吧?

《自由軟體，自由社會》:
自由軟體是關於自由的問題：人們應該擁有對社會有用的所有方式來使用軟體的自由。軟體與有形的物件（像是椅子、三明治和石油）不同，這是因為它可以（在相較之下）非常簡單的方式被複製以及改變。這些可能性使得軟體就如同其本身的特性一般，實用。我們相信軟體的使用者應該能夠加以利用它們。

請參考 GNU 專案之哲學:
http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/philosophy.zh.html

GNU 計畫:
開始於1984年，旨在發展一個 類-Unix 且為 自由軟體 的完整作業系統： GNU 系統。（GNU 是由“GNU's Not Unix”所遞迴定義出的首字母縮寫語；它的發音為“guh-NEW”）。各種使用 Linux 作為內核的 GNU 作業系統正被廣泛地使用著；雖然這些系統通常被稱作為“Linux”，但是它們應該更精確地被稱為 GNU/Linux 系統 。

自由軟體基金會(FSF):
自由軟體基金會是一個致力於募集資金，以使 GNU 計畫得以進行的的免稅慈善機構。

GNU 計畫的哲學和歷史可在理查‧史托曼 的文章、《GNU 計畫》以及許多其他放在 GNU 專案的哲學 章節中的文章找到。

自由軟體基金會支持在網路上的言論、出版以及集會結社自由 、在私人通信上使用加密軟體的權利以及不受私人壟斷所阻礙的軟體編寫權利 。

留住訪客, 從掃除路障做起

來自軟體自由協會 slat-talk mailing list 上的討論, 積丹尼老兄覺得咱們氣象局的網站越來越依靠微軟牌瀏覽器, 原本該給大眾一個方便查詢的工具, 卻變得如此複雜易瀏覽失敗。See www.anybrowser.org for lessons.

而朝陽大學的洪朝貴教授認為我們目前的大眾資訊教育 (特別是製作文件與網頁那部分) 過份強調外觀, 反而忽視內容與功能, 資策會與研考會還有大專院校資訊科系都有責任.

請注意的關點是:
好的廚師用沾醬佐料來 增添 食物的美味;
技術不足的廚師試圖用沾醬佐料來 掩蓋 食物的原味。
好的網頁設計, 用影音特效來 加強 文字的表達效果;
不良的網頁設計, 試圖用影音特效來 取代 文字表達的效果。

我知道該反省了, 但要從何做起呢?

Free Software Club is starting from now!

We are going to start a Free Software Club in YMCA. To be a formal club of YMCA, we need at least five member to register it. For now, we have two members already. One of the members is an active developer of the Taiwan Free Software Community and he used to be an instructor of Red Hat Certified Engineer Courses. Another one is Rick, he is the well known person of computers area in YMCA. We are disappointed there is not any quality computer course in YMCA, so would like to start by ourselves. Basically, we aim to advocate Free Software in the campus, study and share our knowledges and have fun as well.

If you interested, please read the club's charter below:


The YMCA International College Free Software Club's Charter

The Free Software Club is the premiere intellectual forum on campus. We strive to provide stimulating lectures as well as open discussions of free software ranging from esoteric to pragmatic topics, and all points in between.

The Free Software Club's signature event is it's Club "Happy Hours", held at least once a month and open to any member of the Free Software community.

During these meetings the club discusses a variety of topics, mostly based on free software advocacy or research and topics established the week before. At other times, the club will discuss current events and policy concerns, all the while maintaining a strong foothold in Free Software.

The club also tries to provide interesting and topical presentations by YMCA faculty or visiting lecturers, which address current concerns either in the YMCA or the world around us.

We also sponsor events such as barbecues and other get togethers.
